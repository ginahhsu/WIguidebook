Image formats

There are four main types of image formats for the web: PNG, JPG, GIF, and SVG. Use PNG on the WI website, SVG on coded websites, JPG for photos on social media platforms, and GIF for animation.

PNG

PNGs are the most common format for images on the WI website. They are best high quality images, icons and preserving transparency.

  • Lossless: does not lose data during compression
  • Ideal for detailed graphics
  • Supports transparency
  • Supports up to 16 million colours
  • Can be used for static or animation

JPG

JPGs are best for photos as they can support millions of colours. When posting to social media, JPGs are the best option.

  • Lossy: unnecessary information is deleted from the file permanently when compressed
  • Best for real-life images like photographs
  • Best choice for posting images on social media
  • Does not offer transparency

GIF

GIFs were made for small, simple graphic icons that can be animated. There is no sound but are still a good way to bring motion to a site.

  • Lossless: does not lose data during compression
  • Smaller than JPGs
  • Only accomomodate up to 256 indexed colors
  • Can be animated
  • Does not offer transparency

SVG

The newest type of file format. Unlike the others, SVG images will remain crisp and clear at any resolution or size.

  • Lossless: does not lose data during compression
  • Drawn from mathematically declared shapes and curves rather than pixels
  • Can be animated and supports transparency.
  • Large files because they are lossless
Things to note
  • If uploading PNG images to the WI website, be sure to take note of the size as files too large will not upload
  • These are general guidelines on where to use each option, use your best discretion when uploading files
